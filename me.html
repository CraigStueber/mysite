Here are somethings I bring to the table.

Determination

When I decided to go back to school in my 30’s I knew it was going to be hard. I was learning something totally new. Each term I set a goal to be top of my class and throughout all my schooling have kept a 4.0 GPA. I do not have the luxury of working part-time, or taking time off to study but I push myself to reach my goal. On top of that, I have also completed multiple bootcamps and obtained multiple certifications because I am determined to be the best in my field.

Passion

It may have taken me longer than some to realize my passion, but because of that it is more powerful than most people's as I have forged my passion out of hard work and dedication. I have found my joy in getting lost in the code, working hours to make a program just right. Having to set an alarm to make myself shut my laptop at night so I can wake up and take the kids to school in the morning. True passion is something you find and forge and sacrifice for to create the change you want in life.

Work Ethic

I remember my grandfather once told me "I don't care what you are doing. Give it all of yourself." I have always tried to live that way no matter what I am doing I give it everything I have. I always hold myself to the highest degree and push myself to go above and beyond in everything I do.

Problem Solver

Problem solving is a hobby for me and is one of the things that attracted me to coding in the first place. I love to look at a hard problem or issue and work through it with logic and critical thinking to analyze the situation. With a results-oriented mind set I stay focused on getting to the root of the problem. One of the first dates I took my wife on was to an escape room here in RVA, we still go to as many as possible.

Eye For Design

Even though I hear from other programmers that they don't think an artistic mind is needed to code, I have to disagree. Having an eye for design helps when working with UI, as well as creating clean code. Writing code to make it clean is like organizing random lines and making them into a work of art. My art background sets me apart from most programmers because I look at the world in a creative light and apply that to my programs.

Out of the Box Thinker

Having lived my whole life with dyslexia I have taught myself to look at things differently. This give me the ability to go beyond traditional approaches to issues and problems. I had an art professor once say to the class “What do you mean you can’t draw hands? Hands are no different than a jar or a face. Everything is just lines.” When I come upon a problem I think of this statement because nothing is impossible to figure out and everything is just lines waiting to be transformed.

Time Management

Going to an online school has really amplified my ability to manage time and organize my week. Each week I schedule all of my assignments and commitments, then make sure to hold myself accountable and get everything done. On top of school, I have been working full-time and still have time to make dinner and play games with my family, while running a dungeons and dragons game and working on coding projects and my portfolio.

Broad Life Experience

I have traveled all over this wonderful planet even though there is still so much more I want to see. Having chosen this career later in life, I know that software development is what I want to do for the rest of my life. My home and family are here in Richmond, VA, and I am looking for a long-term career I love, that feels like a family as well.
